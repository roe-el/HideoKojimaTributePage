Hideo Kojima

The Man behind Metal Gear

Hideo Kojima is a Japanese video game designer, screenwriter, director, and producer who is regarded for his unique approach to video games. Below is a time line of his work on the award winning and universally acclaimed Metal Gear video game series.
  • 1962-Born in Tokyo, Japan on August 24
  • 1987-Designed and directed Metal Gear for the MSX2 home computer
  • 1990-Designed, directed, and wrote the sequel Metal Gear: Solid Snake
  • 1998-Became a celebrity among the video game industry with the release of Metal Gear Solid
  • 2001-Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty is released to critical acclaim and is highly successful
  • 2004-Kojima designed and released Metal Gear Solid 3: Snake Eater on the Sony Playstation 2
  • 2008-Hideo co-directs Metal Gear Solid 4: Guns of the Patriots with Shuyo Murata
  • 2015-Kojima and Konami release their final Metal Gear franchise game together: Metal Gear Solid V: The Phantom Pain
